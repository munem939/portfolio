Task

For this portfolio task, I undertook an in-depth analysis of Adidas sales data from various retailers in the US. The primary objective was to identify the top-performing stores, assess their sales performance in relation to the set targets, and gain insights into the effectiveness of different sales methods.

The original data contained 9,648 data entries and was obtained from Kaggle. The dataset encompassed sales data from major retailers, including Amazon, Foot Locker, Kohl's, Sports Direct, Walmart, and West Gear. The objective was to assess stores with sales exceeding $50 million, identify a cautionary threshold below $30 million, and examine expenses lost, aiming to keep expenses lost below 55%.

My responsibilities include extracting, cleaning, and transforming Adidas sales data. I conducted statistical analyses to identify performance trends, evaluated in-store and online sales methods, and calculated key performance metrics.

Tools Utilised

Microsoft Excel

Background

Adidas, a leading global sportswear brand, has left an everlasting mark on athletic fashion. With a focus on innovation and performance, it designs clothes across footwear, apparel, and accessories for all ages, genders and sizes. Adidas stands as a powerhouse at the intersection of athleticism and contemporary fashion with a revenue of over $23 billion in 2023.

Data Cleaning and Validation

  • Conducted thorough validation of the Adidas sales dataset to ensure data accuracy and reliability.
  • Checked for the presence of missing values and blanks, confirming a clean dataset.
  • Reviewed retailer IDs to identify and rectify potential discrepancies.
  • Cross-referenced retailer IDs across different retailers to ensure uniqueness.
  • Collaborated with stakeholders and the project team for validation discussions.

Key Findings and Dashboard

Overall Performance Metrics:

  • Total Sales: $899,902,125.00
  • Total Operating Profit: $332,134,761.45
  • Total Expenses: $567,767,363.55
  • Total Expenses Lost: 57.70%

Retailer Operating Profit:

  • Amazon: $28,818,503.31
  • Foot Locker: $80,722,124.81
  • Kohl's: $36,811,252.58
  • Sports Direct: $74,332,954.96
  • Walmart: $25,782,052.61
  • West Gear: $85,667,873.18

Sales Insights:

  • Foot Locker, Sports Direct and West Gear met the operating profit target of over $50 million.
  • Amazon and Walmart are grossly underperforming on reaching their operating profit targets.
  • Dominance of in-store sales for West Gear and outlet sales for Walmart.
  • High expenses lost for in-store Sports Direct and Walmart Sales.
  • Online sales from Amazon, Foot Locker, Kohl's, Sports Direct and West Gear met the minimum expenses target.
  • Online sales method shows lower expenses lost across retailers.

Conclusion

The analysis highlights disparities in retailer operating profits, with Foot Locker, Sports Direct, and West Gear meeting the $50 million target. Amazon and Walmart underperform, emphasizing strategic areas for improvement. Dominance of in-store sales for West Gear and higher expenses lost for in-store sales at Sports Direct and Walmart signal targeted focus areas. Successful online sales across retailers underscore the significance of digital channels in optimizing profitability. These insights provide a foundation for strategic decision-making to enhance retail partnerships and overall performance
